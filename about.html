About the Conference

Bridging Ancient Wisdom to Global Sustainability

Conference Overview

The Conference shall be uniquely organised in the traditional Indian manner, celebrating the Nation's rich cultural heritage. Emphasis shall be on creating an immersive atmosphere that honours India's timeless wisdom and cultural ethos.

Everything from wearing traditional Indian attire, enjoying authentic Indian vegetarian cuisine, to following ancient practices such as lamp lighting, vedic chants, and traditional welcome ceremonies to mark the beginning of each session shall bring a unique experience for every participant.

Also, avail the opportunity to visit the holy cities of Haridwar and Rishikesh and be a part of the devotional vibrance on the banks of river Ganga.

Conference Objectives

Revitalize Ancient Wisdom

To showcase the depth and relevance of India's ancient knowledge systems—spanning Ayurveda, Yoga, Astronomy, Architecture, and more—for solving contemporary global challenges.

Bridge Tradition and Innovation

To foster interdisciplinary research that integrates traditional Indian philosophies with modern science, technology, and policy-making.

Promote Global Dialogue

To provide an international platform for scholars, practitioners, and students to exchange insights and collaborate on the applications of Indian Knowledge Systems.

Inspire Educational Transformation

To encourage the inclusion of IKS in mainstream education, curriculum innovation, and institutional frameworks at national and global levels.

Position India as a Knowledge Leader

To elevate India's intellectual heritage globally and establish its leadership in shaping a sustainable, inclusive, and ethically grounded future.

Expected Outcomes

  • Comprehensive research papers and publications on various aspects of Indian Knowledge Systems
  • Collaborative research initiatives between international institutions and scholars
  • Policy recommendations for integrating traditional knowledge into modern educational curricula
  • Development of innovative methodologies for validating and applying traditional knowledge
  • Creation of a global network of researchers and practitioners in Indian Knowledge Systems
  • Identification of funding opportunities for research projects in traditional knowledge domains
  • Establishment of best practices for preserving and transmitting traditional knowledge

Who can Participate?

  • Corporate Executives/Entrepreneurs/Practitioners.
  • Academicians/Research Scholars/Students.
  • Participation only through physical presence (No online participation)
